Summer 2026 is bringing back some classics and raising the bar on texture. These five styles are setting the tone for men's grooming this season — and Jonathan at True Stylez is already executing them flawlessly.

## 1. The Skin Taper Fade

The ultimate clean look. A clean skin fade on the sides with 1–2 inches of length on top, styled with a light pomade. It's sharp, it breathes, and it's low-maintenance.

**Who it works for:** Oval, diamond, and rectangular faces.

## 2. The Drop Fade

The drop fade curves the fade line downward behind the ear instead of following the natural hairline. It creates a softer profile and is ideal for men with prominent posterior hairlines.

**Who it works for:** Every face shape. This is Jonathan's most recommended style this season.

## 3. The Textured Crop Fade

Keep it short on top with fractional layering, then fade into a tight 0.5–1 guard on the sides. For maximum texture, Jonathan recommends a styling powder on dry hair.

**Who it works for:** Men with wavy or curly hair who want volume without bulk.

## 4. The Burst Fade

The burst fade — named for its semi-circular, bursting shape behind the ear — is a high-fashion take on the classic taper. Paired with a longer curly or afro-textured top, it's art on a head.

**Who it works for:** Round, oval, and heart faces.

## 5. The Mid Fade

A reassuring middle ground. Mid fades cut off at the midpoint of the head — higher than a taper but not skin-deep. It's the most versatile cut in the book and transitions seamlessly into any length on top.

**Who it works for:** Anyone who wants the fade look without the maintenance window.
