Not all fades are created equal. Walk into any barbershop and you'll hear the same two terms thrown around: **taper fade** and **skin fade**. They sound interchangeable, but the barber's blade grade tells a very different story.

## What Is a Taper Fade?

A taper fade gradually shortens the hair from longer at the top to shorter toward the nape and temples — but it **stops short of the skin**. The lowest grade is typically a #1 or #2 clipper guard, leaving a small amount of visible hair at the very bottom.

**Best for:** Men with thick hair who want a clean, natural look that grows out gracefully. A taper fade is low-maintenance and works with every face shape.

## What Is a Skin Fade?

A skin fade takes it all the way down. The barber uses a **detachable blade** or foil shaver to cut the hair completely to the scalp at the fade point, then blends seamlessly upward. The result is a dramatic, crisp silhouette.

**Best for:** Clients who want a high-fashion, sharply defined look. Skin fades are less forgiving as they grow out but look incredible in the moment.

## How to Choose

| Factor              | Taper Fade          | Skin Fade                    |
|---------------------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| Growth-out window   | 3–5 weeks           | 1–2 weeks                    |
| Wrinkles / scalp    | More forgiving      | Very precise                 |
| Face shape fit      | All                 | Oval, diamond, heart best    |
| Barber skill level  | Intermediate        | Advanced                     |
